The Premise

A commission, not a purchase.

Our four signature speakers are built to a set of drawings we have refined for years. Bespoke Professional is different. It begins with a blank sheet, a conversation, and a visit — either yours to our workshop in Antigua, or ours to your room.

We listen to how you listen. We measure the space. We discuss the music that matters to you — the records you return to when the room is empty and the lights are low. Then our lead luthier drafts a speaker designed, voiced, and finished for your room and no other.

Every pair is numbered, signed, and accompanied by a bound dossier: the acoustic analysis of your space, the chosen tonewood with its provenance, the driver selection, and the name of every craftsperson who touched it.

§ The Process

Around three months. One pair. Yours alone.

A bespoke commission is not a faster version of our catalog — it is a slower one. We accept a limited number of commissions each year. The process is deliberate because the object is meant to outlast every other thing in the room.

  • 01 Introduction & room visitA ninety-minute conversation. We bring measurement microphones and questions.
  • 02 Acoustic designYour room's response drives the enclosure, driver choice, and crossover. We send drawings.
  • 03 Material selectionYou choose the tonewood, the grille cloth, the metalwork, the stone. Samples shipped.
  • 04 Commission & craftRoughly three months in the workshop — lead times are estimates, confirmed in the formal quote. Progress photographs every two weeks.
  • 05 VoicingWe burn the speakers in for 200 hours, then voice them against reference recordings.
  • 06 InstallationTwo of us fly to your room, install, level, voice in situ, and stay for the first listening.

§ What changes

Every variable, on the table.

On our catalog, the acoustic decisions are made. On a bespoke commission, nothing is decided until you and the workshop decide it together.

  • FormHorn-loaded, open-baffle, sealed, bass reflex — or a hybrid we design for your room.
  • WoodBeyond our four tonewoods: walnut, sycamore, rosewood, aged Guatemalan cedar, and woods we source on request.
  • Stone & metalSerpentine marble horns, patinated brass, hand-forged iron bases, cast bronze hardware.
  • Leather & fabricHand-stitched saddle leather wraps, Italian suede baffles, woven cane grilles, silk-thread cloths dyed in-house.
  • DriversField-coil, Alnico, AMT, ribbon, compression. We partner with specialists worldwide.
  • ScaleFrom intimate 28-inch towers to room-defining four-way horn systems over six feet tall.
  • FinishFrench polish, hand-rubbed lacquer, oiled satin, or a finish developed for your commission alone.
