## 2. BRAND ESSENCE & PHILOSOPHY

AURALIS is a luxury bespoke hi-fi speaker company rooted in Guatemala. It is not a mass-market audio brand — it is a small-batch workshop that builds speakers by hand from sustainably sourced Central American hardwoods, using woodworking techniques inherited from generations of Mayan artisans.

### The Core Idea
The ancient Maya understood acoustics in ways modern science is only now rediscovering. Their pyramids were engineered to produce echoes that mimicked the call of the quetzal bird. Their ceremonial plazas amplified the human voice across impossible distances. Sound, for the Maya, was not entertainment — it was sacred architecture.

AURALIS carries this legacy forward. Every speaker we build begins as a raw slab of Guatemalan hardwood — the same species used for centuries to build the marimba, Guatemala's national instrument. We shape these tonewoods using traditional joinery (no screws, no staples, no particle board), then pair them with precision-engineered European drivers and custom crossover networks designed in-house.

The result is a loudspeaker that sounds different from anything built in a factory — because it IS different. The wood itself becomes part of the acoustic system. Its grain, its density, its natural oils all shape the sound. No two pairs are identical. Each one carries the fingerprint of the forest, the hands that shaped it, and a thousand years of acoustic knowledge.

### Brand Voice & Personality
- **Warm, not corporate.** We speak like a craftsperson talking about their work — proud, knowledgeable, grounded. Never salesy or hype-driven.
- **Culturally proud, not performative.** The Mayan heritage is real and central — not a marketing angle. We honor it with specificity and depth.
- **Technical but accessible.** We know the science inside and out, but we explain it in human terms. We never talk down to anyone.
- **Luxury through authenticity.** Our prices reflect the materials and labor. We never apologize for cost — we explain the craft behind it.
- **Sensory language.** We describe sound the way sommeliers describe wine — with texture, warmth, space, weight.

### Who We Serve
- Audiophiles who value craftsmanship and provenance over brand logos
- Design-conscious homeowners who want their sound system to be furniture-grade beautiful
- Vinyl collectors and music lovers who listen actively, not passively
- People building dedicated listening rooms or upgrading from mid-fi to end-game systems
- Buyers who appreciate the story behind what they own

## 5. TONEWOOD LIBRARY

### Hormigo
- **Origin:** Guatemalan Highlands
- **Acoustic Character:** Warm, resonant lows with singing sustain
- **Story:** Hormigo is the sacred tonewood of the marimba — Guatemala's national instrument. Its cellular structure creates natural resonance chambers that amplify warmth and sustain. Each slab is air-dried for 24 months in the highland climate before entering the workshop.
- **Mayan Heritage:** Used by the K'iche' Maya for ceremonial instruments for over 1,000 years.

### Granadillo
- **Origin:** Petén Lowlands
- **Acoustic Character:** Tight, controlled, with precise imaging
- **Story:** Granadillo's density rivals ebony, but its grain tells a richer story — streaks of amber and chocolate through deep burgundy. Mayan woodworkers called it the "blood wood" for its color. Its rigidity makes the cabinet nearly inert — only the drivers move.
- **Mayan Heritage:** Traditionally carved into Mayan ceremonial masks and royal staffs.

### Conacaste
- **Origin:** Pacific Lowlands
- **Acoustic Character:** Open, airy, with natural spaciousness
- **Story:** Guatemala's beloved Conacaste grows slowly into massive canopies. Its wood is light yet rigid — ideal for open-baffle designs where the cabinet must be acoustically transparent. Each slab carries the golden hue of Guatemalan sunlight.
- **Mayan Heritage:** Known as the "ear tree" — its seed pods were used as rattles in Mayan music.

### Cocobolo
- **Origin:** Highland Cloud Forests
- **Acoustic Character:** Bright, articulate, with harmonic complexity
- **Story:** Cocobolo is among the world's most prized tonewoods — dense, oily, and shimmering with color. Its natural resins act as a vibration dampener, making it self-deadening. Each piece is unique — a living fingerprint of the forest.
- **Mayan Heritage:** Reserved for royal artisans; used in Mayan wind instruments.

### PAGE 5: AI Sound Consultation (The Core Experience)

**Purpose:** This is the flagship interactive feature — an AI-powered chat consultant that guides the customer through a personalized speaker recommendation and can take orders.

**The Experience Flow:**
1. User arrives at the page and sees a warm invitation to begin: "Let us curate your sound." Brief description of what the consultation involves. A prominent "Begin Consultation" button.

2. Upon clicking, a chat interface opens. The AI consultant (powered by Claude) greets the customer warmly and begins the guided conversation.

3. **Step 1 — The Room:** The consultant asks about the customer's listening space: room size (small / medium / large), room shape, flooring type (hardwood, carpet, tile, concrete), any acoustic challenges (echo, open plan, shared walls), and where the speakers will be placed.

4. **Step 2 — Listening Style:** How does the customer listen? Nearfield at a desk? Relaxed from a couch across the room? Background ambiance while working? Critical, focused listening sessions? Late-night low-volume? Loud and immersive?

5. **Step 3 — Music & Sources:** What genres dominate? Who are their favorite artists? Do they listen primarily to vinyl, digital files, streaming, or a mix? This matters because different speaker designs favor different source material.

6. **Step 4 — Existing System:** Does the customer already have an amplifier? What sources (turntable, DAC, streamer)? What's their budget range? Are they starting from scratch or upgrading a component?

7. **Step 5 — The Recommendation:** Based on all inputs, the consultant recommends 1–2 AURALIS speakers with a detailed, personalized explanation: why that wood, why those drivers, why that form factor — tied directly to the customer's room, music, and listening style. The consultant references the Mayan heritage and the wood's acoustic character naturally.

8. **Step 6 — Order Flow:** If the customer wants to proceed, the consultant collects: full name, email address, shipping city/country, confirms the speaker model and price, and any customization requests. The consultant then provides a summary and next steps (the AURALIS team will follow up with a formal invoice and shipping timeline).

**AI Consultant Personality:**
- Warm, knowledgeable, conversational — like talking to a passionate craftsperson
- Never condescending or salesy
- Culturally proud — references the Mayan heritage and Guatemalan origin naturally, not performatively
- Concise per message (2–4 sentences) — guides step by step, never dumps all questions at once
- Uses sensory language when describing sound (texture, warmth, space, weight, air)
- Comfortable saying "I don't know" or "that's a great question — let me think about that"

**Technical Implementation:**
- Powered by Claude API (model: claude-sonnet-4-20250514)
- System prompt contains: full product catalog with specs, all tonewood stories, brand voice guidelines, step-by-step consultation flow, order collection instructions
- Conversation history is maintained throughout the session
- Chat UI includes: AURALIS wave icon in header, "Auralis" label on assistant messages, typing indicator, clean input field

## 9. PROMPT INSTRUCTION FOR CLAUDE

When building any page or component for the AURALIS website, follow these guidelines:

1. **Always use the exact color palette defined above.** The teal (#6B9B96) is the hero accent. The cream (#F0EBE3) is the dominant background. Never use generic purples, blues, or grays that don't match the brand.

2. **Typography must feel editorial and premium.** Outfit for display, Libre Baskerville for serif moments, IBM Plex Mono for technical labels. Never use Inter, Arial, Roboto, or system fonts.

3. **The Mayan geometric motifs are subtle, not loud.** They appear at 5–12% opacity as decorative accents — borders, corner elements, section dividers. They are abstracted step-fret geometries, not literal Mayan glyphs or calendar symbols.

4. **The wave icon from the logo is a recurring brand element.** Use it in navigation, section headers, the chat interface, and the footer. It is the visual thread that ties the experience together.

5. **Copy tone is warm, grounded, and specific.** Reference specific woods by name. Reference Mayan culture with respect and specificity. Use sensory language for sound descriptions. Never use generic marketing language like "premium quality" or "cutting-edge technology."

6. **The AI consultation is the crown jewel.** It must feel like a conversation with a real person who cares about your listening experience — not a chatbot. The system prompt must contain the full product catalog, wood stories, and brand voice to deliver knowledgeable, personalized recommendations.

7. **Light aesthetic, not dark.** The AURALIS brand is warm and natural. The default is cream/warm white backgrounds, not dark mode. The only dark surface is the footer.

8. **Space is luxury.** Generous padding, generous line-height, generous margins. Never crowd the layout. Let the content breathe like an open-baffle speaker.
