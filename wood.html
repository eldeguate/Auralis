03 — Technical Philosophy

Why wood outperforms MDF.

MDF is consistent, cheap, and dead. That last property is what speaker designers actually prize — a cabinet that does not move. But "dead" and "natural" are not the same thing.

Solid tonewood damps itself through cellular structure and resin. It moves just enough, at frequencies well outside the audible band, to warm the sound without coloring it. The result isn't neutrality. It's presence.

That presence is the reason a marimba does not sound like a xylophone. Same bars. Same mallets. Different wood. Our cabinets work the same way.

04 — Joinery

As beautiful inside as out.

We use traditional Mayan and European woodworking techniques inherited from our luthier lineage: mortise-and-tenon for primary joints, finger joints for panels, hide glue for acoustic transparency.

Open any AURALIS cabinet and you will find the same care on the inside as the outside. No particle board. No staples. No sloppy bracing. A speaker you would be proud to see disassembled.
